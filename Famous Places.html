Bagan

One of the best place to visit in myanmar. Bagan is a ancient city built around the 12th century, it was once home to over 20,000 Buddhist pagodas. Now around 1000 of which remain today. It located on the bands of Irrawaddy(Ayeyarwaddy) River in Mandalay region in Central Myanmar.

Shwedagon Pagoda

The Shwedagon is the most sacred Buddhist pagoda in Myanmar, as it is believed to contain relics of the four previous Buddhas of the present kalpa.Built on the 51 metre ( 167 ft high) above sea level. located in Singuttara Hill in the center of Yangon ( Rangoon)

Inn-lay Lake

Inle Lake is in the Shan Hills of Myanmar. It is the second largest lake in Myanmar. The lake is surrounded by mountains on all sides offering a breathtaking natural view. The area is rich in animals and plant species and you can see some of the rarest species here. It located at the heart of the country.

Golden Rock

Kyi thite yoe is Buddhist worship place in Myanmar. One of the most popular destinations for tourists. It located in Mon State, Myanmar. Visitors can enjoy the beautiful view from the top of Mount Kyaitiyo and the sunset is so lovely.

Chaung Thar Beach

Chaung Thar Beach Myanmar is one of the popular beaches in Myanmar.Chauny thar is a town and beach resort. It is a popular resort and it located in Shwethaunyan Subtown Ship, Pathein Township and it is a popular resort.

Mount Zwegabin

It is the famous landmark of Karen state. The closest major city is Hpa-an,8.2 km northwest of Mount Zwegabin. Mount Zwegabin is the highest point in the region. The surroundings around the mountains are a mosaic of agricultural land.

U Bein Bridge

A bridge which is the oldest teak bridge in the world is made form the remains of a royal palace. The bridge is 1.2-kilometre long and it built around 1850. It located in Mandalay region, Taungthaman Lake near Amarapura in Myanmar. The bridge is beautiful and popular place to capture the beauty of Myanmar.

Mandalay Hill

Mandalay Hill is a 240 metres (790 ft) hill that is located to the northeast of the city centre of Mandalay in Myanmar. The King Mindom min founded mandalay in 1857 and he built the royal palace as the first place in Mandalay from 1857 to 1859. It is surrounded by four 2km-long walls with a total of 48 turrets and 12 gates which is a symbol of 12 months per year.

Pindaya Cave

The southernmost Pindaya cave can be entered and extends for about 490 feet along a well-worn path.It is known for its interior which contains over 8,000 images of Buddha. Some of the older statues and images in the cave have inscriptions dating to the late 18th century, or early Konbaung period, and the earliest one dates from 1773.
